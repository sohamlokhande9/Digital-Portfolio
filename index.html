About Me

I'm a first-year Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering student at MIT Academy of Engineering in Alandi, Pune. I've always been intensely curious about how things work under the hood, which naturally pulled me toward the intersection of hardware and software.

Whether I'm writing C++ logic or wiring up a circuit on a breadboard, I love the messy, rewarding process of bringing an idea to life. I don't just want to build things; I want to understand them from the ground up.

When I'm not studying tech or acting as the admin for our 'Circuit Breakers' group, you'll probably find me exploring my passion for art, playing the tabla professionally, or collaborating with the Goonj Unplugged club. I believe engineering and art are deeply connected—they both require rhythm, curiosity, and a lot of practice.
